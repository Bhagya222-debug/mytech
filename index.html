Understanding Reported Speech

Introduction Video

Watch this video to understand the basics of reported speech before diving into the examples below.

What is Reported Speech?

Reported Speech (also called Indirect Speech) is when we tell someone what another person said, but we don't use their exact words.

When we change from direct speech to reported speech, we need to make some changes:

  • Tense Changes: Past becomes past perfect, present becomes past
  • Pronoun Changes: "I" becomes "he/she", "you" becomes "I/we"
  • Time/Place Changes: "today" becomes "that day", "here" becomes "there"
  • Remove Quotation Marks: Add "that" after the reporting verb

Interactive Animated Example

Sarah

"I am studying English grammar."

Transform to Reported Speech

Teacher

Sarah said that she was studying English grammar.

Step-by-Step Transformation:

1 Remove quotation marks
2 Add "that" after "said"
3 Change "I" to "she"
4 Change "am" to "was"

More Examples

Can you remember what Uncle Angelo said?

Example 1: Present to Past

Direct Speech: Uncle Angelo said, "It looks very nice"
Reported Speech: Uncle Angelo said that it looked very nice.
"looks" → "looked" Added "that"

Example 2: Pronoun Changes

Direct Speech: "He is my new boss", Uncle Angelo said.
Reported Speech: Uncle Angelo said that he was his new boss.
"is" → "was" "my" → "his"
Grammar Rule: Present tense in direct speech changes to past tense in reported speech.

Example 3: Past to Past Perfect

Direct Speech: Uncle Angelo said, "I decided to shave it off"
Reported Speech: Uncle Angelo said that he had decided to shave it off.
"decided" → "had decided" "I" → "he"
Grammar Rule: Past tense in direct speech changes to past perfect tense in reported speech.
